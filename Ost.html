Openings & Endings

Season 1 (Openings and Closings)


The original Hajime no Ippo series features a variety of memorable openings and closings that set the tone for the episodes and reflect the protagonist’s journey. The first opening,"Under Star" by Shocking Lemon, captures the determination and promise of Ippo’s early days as he steps into the world of boxing. This is followed by "Inner Light" by Shocking Lemon, which continues the energetic and motivational vibe in episodes 26–52, highlighting Ippo's ongoing growth and challenges. The opening "Tumbling Dice"by Tsuneo Imahori, used from episodes 53–76, brings a faster and more intense feel, mirroring the tension and excitement of Ippo’s most significant battles. The closings are equally impactful, starting with "Yūzora no Kami Hikōki" by Mori Naoya, which offers a calm and reflective end to the first half of the season, emphasizing moments of personal growth."360°" by Mori Naoya, used for episodes 26–52, 75, and the OVA, maintains an uplifting tone that fits Ippo’s continued perseverance. The final closing,"Eternal Loop" by SABER TIGER, featured in episodes 53–74 and 76, is a powerful, rock-infused track that encapsulates the series’ themes of resilience and the relentless pursuit of improvement. These openings and closings seamlessly transition between the high stakes of Ippo's fights and quieter moments of introspection, enhancing the overall storytelling.


Season 1 Opening 1: Under Star by "Shocking Lemon"

Season Ending 1: Yūzora no Kami Hikōki by "Mori Naoya"

Season 1 Opening 2: Inner Light by "Shocking Lemon"

Season 1 Ending 2: 360° by "Mori Naoya"

Season 1 Opening 3: Tumbling Dice by "Tsuneo Imahori"

Season 1 Ending 3: Eternal Loop by "SABER TIGER"

Season 2 (Openings and Closing)


In Hajime no Ippo: New Challenger, the music continues to deliver impactful themes that enhance the storytelling. The opening theme, "Hekireki" by Last Alliance (Episodes 1–26), is an electrifying track that captures the intense energy and determination of Ippo’s journey in this sequel. Its fast-paced rhythm and gripping lyrics reflect the fierce competition and high stakes of the boxing world. The closing theme, "8AM" by Coldrain (Episodes 1–26), provides a more reflective and emotive contrast, offering a moment of calm and introspection after each episode. With its heartfelt melody and lyrics, it underscores the struggles and growth of the characters, leaving viewers inspired and ready for more. Together, these themes create a balanced emotional arc that complements the action-packed and motivational spirit of the series.

- Season 2 Opening: Hekireki by "Last Alliance"

- Season 2 Ending: 8AM by "Coldrain"

Season 3 (Openings and Closing)


In Hajime no Ippo: Rising, the music continues to elevate the series’ emotional depth and intensity. The opening theme, "Yakan Hikō" (夜間飛行, lit. Night Flight) by Tsuneo Imahori (Episodes 1–25), delivers a moody yet electrifying atmosphere that sets the stage for Ippo’s challenges in the ring. With its compelling melody and evocative energy, it reflects both the anticipation of battles and the introspective journey of the characters. The closing theme,"Buchikome!!" (ブチコメ!!, lit. Smash It!!) by Shikuramen (Episodes 1–25), contrasts with a more upbeat and motivational tone, celebrating the spirit of never giving up. Its lively rhythm and bold lyrics resonate with Ippo’s determination to push forward no matter the odds. Together, these themes capture the highs and lows of the series, amplifying the drama, action, and perseverance that define Hajime no Ippo.

- Season 3 Opening: Yakan Hikō by "Tsuneo Imahori"

- Season 3 Ending: Buchikome!! by "Shikuramen"
